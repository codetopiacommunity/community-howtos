# Ways to Contribute

Contributing to Codetopia Community is not just about writing code.
Whatever your skills, there is a place for you here. This guide walks you
through every way you can get involved.

> [!NOTE]
> All roles in the community are voluntary and unpaid, including
> department leads. What you gain is experience, mentorship, and a
> public track record of your work.

---
## Who can contribute?

**On GitHub: anyone can help.** Our repos are public. If you spot a bug,
a typo, a broken link, or something that no longer works the way the docs
say it does, you can report it or fix it yourself. You do not need to be
a member to make something better.

**Beyond that, you need to be a member.** Joining a department, writing
for our blog, mentoring, volunteering at events, and writing new howtos
are all for verified members of the community. These need context that
only comes from being part of what we are building.

**Contributing on GitHub but not a member yet?** Join us. You will be able
to talk directly with maintainers, have your work recognized inside the
community, and unlock every other way to contribute on this page. It takes
a few minutes.

🔗 [Join the community](../Getting-Started/01-join-the-community)

## Quick contributions

These are things you can do today, no long-term commitment needed.

### Fix something on GitHub

Spotted a typo in our docs? A broken link? A bug on the website? You can
report it or fix it yourself, even if you have never contributed to a
project before. Our guides walk you through it step by step.

🔗 [Contributing on GitHub](#)

### Write something

Writing comes in two forms, and you can do either or both. They are
different jobs, so here is how to tell them apart.

**Howtos** are practical guides and walkthroughs for getting things done
in Codetopia Community. How to join, how to use Discord, how to
contribute to a project, how to submit your work. If a member is stuck on
something about how this community works, a howto is what unblocks them.
The guide you are reading right now is one. They live in our
<a href="https://github.com/codetopiacommunity/community-howtos" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">community-howtos</a>
repo.

- **Who writes them?** Members, mostly. Writing a good howto means
  knowing what people around here actually get stuck on, and that comes
  from being part of the community.
- **Can anyone fix one?** Yes. If you spot a typo, a broken link, or a
  step that no longer matches what you see on screen, open a pull
  request. You do not need to be a member to make something better.
- **How?** Through GitHub, the same way as code.
- **What happens next?** We review it, and once it is merged it becomes
  part of this collection.

🔗 [Contributing on GitHub](#)

**Technical articles** are teaching. They answer "help me understand
this" rather than "how do I do this." Tutorials, deep dives, lessons
learned, things you figured out the hard way. People read them to learn
something, not to unblock themselves in the next five minutes. They are
longer, they carry your voice, and they are published on our Hashnode
blog under Codetopia Community's name.

- **Who writes them?** Members of the **Writers** team.
- **How?** Create a portal account, then request to join the Writers
  team. Once you are approved, you can start writing.
- **What should I put in my request?** Link something you have written
  before, anywhere. A blog post, an article, a howto, anything that
  shows how you write.

🔗 <a href="https://portal.community.codetopia.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">portal.community.codetopia.org</a>

**Still not sure which one you are writing?** Ask yourself who is reading
it and why. If they are stuck and want it fixed, it is a howto. If they
are curious and want to understand, it is an article.

## Join a department

If you want to contribute in an ongoing way, our departments are where the
real work happens. Each one is a team of members who keep a part of the
community running. Read through them and see where you fit.

> [!NOTE]
> On the portal, departments are called **teams**. Same thing, two names.

- **Mentorship and member growth**, guiding members one-on-one and helping
  them level up. *This is for you if you enjoy seeing someone else win
  because of your support.*

- **Learning**, building learning content, curriculum, and study programs.
  *This is for you if you like breaking hard things down so beginners can
  understand them.*

- **Research and insights**, understanding our members, what they need,
  and how the community is doing. *This is for you if you like asking
  questions, gathering data, and finding out what is really going on.*

- **Comms, branding and marketing**, how Codetopia Community looks,
  sounds, and reaches people. *This is for you if you care about design,
  storytelling, or getting the word out.*

- **Projects and open source**, driving our open source projects and
  coordinating the people who contribute to them. *This is for you if you
  like keeping open source work moving and helping contributors ship.*

- **Events**, planning and running community events, online and in
  person, all year round. *This is for you if you like bringing people
  together and making things happen behind the scenes.*

- **Partnership**, building relationships with organizations, companies,
  and other communities. *This is for you if you are a connector and
  enjoy opening doors.*

- **Operations**, keeping the community machinery running day to day.
  *This is for you if you are organized and hate watching things fall
  through the cracks.*

- **Technical**, the infrastructure behind the community: the portal,
  website, bots, and tooling. *This is for you if you want to build and
  maintain real systems that hundreds of members use every day.*

### How to join a department

Log in to the portal, open the team you want to join, and click
**Request to join**. A team lead will review your request and get you
started.

Not sure which one fits you? Ask in **#GENERAL-CHANNEL-PLACEHOLDER** on
Discord and someone will point you in the right direction.

🔗 <a href="https://portal.community.codetopia.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">portal.community.codetopia.org</a>
